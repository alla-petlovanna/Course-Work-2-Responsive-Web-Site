About Me

My name is Alla. I have been knitting for many years. My grandmother showed me how to knit, and I watched a lot of YouTube videos. The first thing that I knitted was a long scarf. Then I knitted a v-neck vest, a skirt, a sweater, two pairs of socks, and another scarf.

Knitting is my way of relaxing. It helps me in difficult times. Usually when I knit, I watch something on my laptop. I like to watch series, films, and some videos.

Knitting is undoubtedly a form of love. I like to knit for the people I love. Every inch of yarn went through my hands, and when my loved ones wear the clothes I knitted, it is like my hug.

Knitting

Knitting is a fiber art in which loops of yarn are interlocked with the assistance of specially designed needles. Usually, two needles and a ball of yarn are enough for knitting. There are several different types of knitting. It is also a very versatile craft that can produce a wide variety of end products. Socks, sweaters, scarves, gloves, and hats are common garments that lend themselves well to knitting.

There is a wide variety of needles. They differ in type, material, length, and width. I prefer to use ADDI metal needles with lace for garments like sweaters, scarfs and so on. Knitters need five short needles to knit one sock. I prefer to use KnitPro bamboo needles for socks.

Yarn also comes in a dizzying range, from simple worsted wool to novelty yarns. In addition to acrylic, yarn can be made from a wide variety of animal and plant fibers such as wool, silk, cotton, bamboo, soy, or corn, and it can be smooth, fuzzy, dyed, or plain. I prefer to use merino yarn for sweaters, vests, and socks. For summer clothes - cotton yarn. I use Alize mixed yarn for scarfs and decor.

There are many additional tools for knitting, such as a row counter, an additional needle for creating braid cables, and so on. They are not required, but make life easier for the knitter.
